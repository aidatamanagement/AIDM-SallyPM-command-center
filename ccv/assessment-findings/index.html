Core Finding:

CCV has outgrown its current technology stack. The systems that supported the organization through its growth phase are now creating friction, duplicate work, and data reliability issues. Current Systems Review

CCV relies on five primary systems. Each was evaluated for functionality, reliability, integration capability, and alignment with organizational goals.

Theodore (SGO Platform) Critical Issues

Theodore is the external-facing platform that powers the Student Government Organization program across 25+ states. It handles student registration, chapter management, scoring, and payment processing.

Identified Problems
  • Multi-factor authentication required on every single login, creating friction for all users
  • Manual scoring processes that should be automated
  • Stripe payment integration issues causing failed transactions
  • Duplicate receipt generation creating confusion for parents and advisors
  • Financial reporting shows inaccurate numbers -- dashboard figures do not match actual revenue
  • "Mystery numbers" appearing on the dashboard with no clear data source
  • No mobile-responsive design for students and advisors in the field
  • Limited self-service capabilities for chapter advisors
Assessment
Recommendation: Full Replacement

Theodore cannot be incrementally improved. The codebase, architecture, and user experience all need to be rebuilt from scratch. The replacement (SGO Web App) will serve students, advisors, chapter leaders, and state coordinators through three distinct portals with role-based access.

Virtuous (CRM) Critical Issues

Virtuous serves as CCV's donor and contact management system, holding approximately 49,000 records and managing 3,365 email contacts for outreach.

Identified Problems
  • Staff describe it as "killing" them in terms of daily workflow friction
  • Integration with SGO/Theodore is completely broken
  • Email automation limited to linear drip sequences only
  • No branching logic or behavioral triggers for communications
  • 49,000 records need deduplication and data hygiene
  • Reporting capabilities do not meet leadership needs
Assessment
Recommendation: Full Rebuild

Virtuous has reached its limits for CCV's needs. The CRM will be rebuilt as part of the CCV Command Center, with purpose-built donor management, advanced email automation with behavioral triggers, and full integration with both the SGO Web App and department dashboards. All 49,000 records will be migrated, deduplicated, and cleaned as part of the transition.

Cornerstone Data Migration

Payment processing platform. Transaction history and donor records need to be migrated into the new system architecture. Currently operates separately from Theodore, creating reconciliation issues.

Pathwright LMS Platform

Learning management system used for training content. Course data, completion records, and user progress will be migrated into the new platform. Training functionality will be rebuilt within the Command Center.

Growth Zone Events/Membership

Event management and membership tracking. Event data and member records require migration. Functionality will be absorbed into the Command Center's department dashboards.

Data Migration Scope:

Three separate data migrations required (Cornerstone, Pathwright, Growth Zone) plus 4+ years of Theodore/SGO historical data. All migrations will preserve historical records and be validated before any system cutover.

Key Findings

Beyond individual system issues, our assessment uncovered organization-wide patterns that affect productivity, accuracy, and growth potential.

Data Reliability Crisis

Multiple staff members reported not trusting the numbers in their systems. Theodore displays a significant discrepancy between displayed and actual revenue figures. Dashboard figures have no traceable source. Financial reporting requires manual reconciliation across multiple systems before any number can be trusted. This erodes confidence in data-driven decision making.

Manual Process Overload

Staff across departments spend significant time on tasks that should be automated: manual scoring, duplicate data entry, cross-system reconciliation, report compilation, and email list management. These manual processes are not just inefficient but also introduce human error.

System Fragmentation

Five core systems that do not communicate effectively with each other. Staff operate in silos, re-entering the same information in multiple places. There is no single source of truth for organizational metrics, making leadership reporting labor-intensive and often delayed.

Compliance and Reporting Gaps

As a nonprofit operating across 25+ states with political advocacy work, CCV has significant compliance obligations. Current systems do not adequately support state-by-state regulatory tracking, financial transparency requirements, or the audit trails needed for organizational governance.

Growth Bottleneck

SGO is expanding nationally, but Theodore cannot scale to support new states, increased student volume, or the operational complexity of multi-state coordination. The current platform is the primary bottleneck to SGO's growth strategy.

Communication Infrastructure

Email marketing is limited to basic drip campaigns through Virtuous. There is no behavioral segmentation, no automated journeys based on user actions, and limited ability to coordinate messaging across the organization's different audiences (donors, parents, advisors, students, legislators).

Proposed Plan

Based on our findings, we propose building two purpose-built products supported by AI leadership and training. Here is exactly what we will deliver and how.

Product 1: SGO Web App Full Theodore Replacement

A modern, mobile-responsive web application that replaces Theodore entirely. Built for students, advisors, chapter leaders, and state coordinators across 25+ states with plans for national expansion.

Three User Portals
  • Student Portal -- Registration, chapter participation, scoring, resources, and progress tracking
  • Advisor Portal -- Chapter management, student oversight, event coordination, and reporting
  • State Coordinator Portal -- Multi-chapter oversight, state-level analytics, compliance tracking, and resource distribution
Core Capabilities
  • Automated scoring system replacing all manual processes
  • Stripe payment integration with accurate financial reporting
  • AI-powered chatbot for student and advisor support
  • Real-time dashboards with trustworthy data
  • Mobile-first responsive design
  • Multi-state expansion support (25 states to national)
  • 4+ years of historical data migrated from Theodore

Product 2: CCV Command Center Internal Operations Hub

A centralized internal platform that gives CCV leadership and department heads real-time visibility, workflow automation, and data-driven decision support across the entire organization.

Four Core Pillars
Pillar 1: SGO Administration

Internal admin panel for the SGO Web App. Chapter approvals, state management, scoring oversight, financial reconciliation, and advisor management.

Pillar 2: Executive Dashboard

Organization-wide KPIs, financial health, program metrics, staff performance, and cross-department analytics. One screen, trusted numbers.

Pillar 3: Department Dashboards

Dedicated dashboards for each of CCV's 9 departments. Customized workflows, team-specific metrics, and automated task management tailored to each team.

Pillar 4: Compliance Hub

State-by-state regulatory tracking, audit trails, financial transparency reporting, and nonprofit governance tools for 25+ state operations.

Additional Modules
  • CRM Integration -- Connect to existing or new CRM for unified contact and donor management
  • Email Marketing Engine -- Behavioral segmentation, automated journeys, multi-audience campaigns
  • Analytics and Reporting -- AI-powered insights, automated report generation, custom dashboards
  • Data Migrations -- Pathwright (LMS), Growth Zone (events), and Cornerstone (payments) data brought into unified system
